Why Computer Science?

My first introduction to computers came from my dad, who loved taking apart and building computers from scratch. Every time he would get to work, I would be next to him, asking questions and handing him the wrong tools even if he never asked for one. When I was 11, my dad introduced me to Scratch, a simple drag-and-drop style method of "coding" created by MIT and aimed at curious kids like me. I made my first game in Scratch, a simple platformer, and from then on it was off to the races. I learned Python in High School, and make more games and apps along the way. I used those programming skills in my school's Robotics Team to program the movement of my team's robot so that it could be remote controlled.

I study Computer Science because I have been surrounded by technology all my life. From watching my dad build computers to building my own robot for a competition, I saw just how varied and amazing the world of programming can be. And that is why I love to code - everyday is a new challenge for me to broaden my horizons and for me to learn something new.
